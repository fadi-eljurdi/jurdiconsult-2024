Saudi Arabia - A Prominent Investor in MEA?s Gaming Industry

Our fast paced modern times have no time for rest as technology is on a never-ending race; with the rival being itself.


The gaming industry has become significantly pivotal, outperforming the entertainment industry. It has simply become so alluring and has manifested its importance through the increase in gaming gurus, gaming events, and hype when a triple A title is released.


Investing in the gaming industry is not just about entertainment; it is an investment in a dynamic, innovative and culturally influential force that shapes the way we interact with technology and each other. This trend makes it a promising and strategic choice for forward-looking investors.


In an attempt to diversify the resilience of its economy, Saudi Arabia has commenced on a quest to utilize an activity that could be a replacement for the sole dependence on oil. Having recognized that the gaming industry has been on a steady rise ever since the late nineties, Saudi decided to invest heavily in this emerging sector; thus relying on it as part of its extensive economic diversification strategy.


In the recent past years, gaming had taken center-stage in Saudi Arabia, thus it has been on a methodical plan to prove itself as a prominent player in the global gaming industry. As a result, in late 2017, Saudi Arabia established The Saudi Arabian Federation for Electronic and Intellectual Sports (SAFEIS) which has a sole purpose of developing and improving the gaming community and industry. (SAFEIS) strives to showcase Saudi Arabia as a global center point.


In the last final months of the previous year, Saudi Arabia introduced an extensive strategy with an end-goal of advancing the development of the gaming industry (Esports).


According to the Financial Times report, Saudi Arabia had invested a sum of 8 million US dollars in the gaming industry, as it has acquired shares in international companies.


To establish itself as a conspicuous gaming hub for the gaming community, it also hosted an array of lucrative gaming tournaments that were broadcasted live across various platforms. These tournaments not only display the Kingdom's eagerness and dedication to gaming and overall esports industry, but also contribute through giving an excellent platform for gaming enthusiasts from all across the globe to come together and engage in a competitive gaming environment.


Having taken place in Riyadh, and hosted by Gamers8, a tournament called ?The Land of Heroes? brought together 16 of the world's best teams for a Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO) tournament. Saudi Arabian Esports Federation and Valve Corp. The arranged event took place from 16 to 20 August. The tournament had a prize pool of $1 million.


Other neighboring Gulf countries have also been following the footsteps of Saudi Arabia as they have been looking to engage and invest in the gaming industry. Dubai for instance, hosted an esports festival in June 2023 with more than 26,000 visitors. Additionally, The Qatar Investment Authority had acquired a minority share in Monument Sports & Entertainment, which is the owner of the Washington Wizards and Capitals. Kuwait's gaming market is also projected to bloom and grow by over 7% by the end of 2027 to total a sum of 145.50 million US Dollars.
